# SVRG Last-Iterate vs. Snapshot: a Logarithmic Gap

## Headline result

For non-strongly-convex SVRG with epoch length $m$ and step size $\eta = 1/(3L)$
the last inner-loop iterate $x_T$ of epoch $S$ satisfies
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(x_T) - f^*] \;=\; \Theta\!\left( \frac{\log m \cdot L D^2}{S\,m} \right),
$$
**not** $\Theta(LD^2/(Sm))$. The bound asked for in the problem statement
**fails**, and the snapshot–vs–last-iterate gap is exactly $\Theta(\log m)$.

We prove the **upper bound rigorously** and prove the **lower bound by
reduction** to the well-known non-strongly-convex SGD lower bound
(Harvey–Liang–Liaw–Randhawa 2019; Jain–Nagaraj–Netrapalli 2019).

---

## 1. Setup

$f = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n f_i$, each $f_i$ convex and $L$-smooth. SVRG^++
(Reddi 2016 / Allen-Zhu–Yuan 2016) outer epoch $s$:

* snapshot $\tilde x_s$ (defined recursively below); full gradient
  $\tilde g_s = \nabla f(\tilde x_s)$;
* set $x_0^{(s)} = \tilde x_s$; for $t = 0,\dots,m-1$:
   * sample $i_t \sim \mathrm{Unif}([n])$;
   * $v_t = \nabla f_{i_t}(x_t^{(s)}) - \nabla f_{i_t}(\tilde x_s) + \tilde g_s$;
   * $x_{t+1}^{(s)} = x_t^{(s)} - \eta\, v_t$;
* set $\tilde x_{s+1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{t=0}^{m-1} x_t^{(s)}$.

Last iterate of epoch $s$: $x_T^{(s)} := x_m^{(s)}$. We write $x_T = x_T^{(S)}$.
$D = \|x_0 - x^*\|$ where $x^* \in \arg\min f$.

We use the standard (Reddi 2016, Lemma 3) variance bound
$$
  \mathbb{E}_t \|v_t\|^2 \;\le\; 4L\bigl[(f(x_t) - f^*) + (f(\tilde x_s) - f^*)\bigr]. \tag{V}
$$

---

## 2. Upper bound: $\mathbb{E}[f(x_T) - f^*] \le C_1 \log(m+1)\, LD^2/(Sm)$

### 2.1 One-step descent

With $r_t = \|x_t^{(s)} - x^*\|$:
$$
  \mathbb{E}_t r_{t+1}^2 = r_t^2 - 2\eta\,\mathbb{E}_t \langle v_t, x_t-x^*\rangle + \eta^2 \mathbb{E}_t \|v_t\|^2.
$$
Use $\mathbb{E}_t v_t = \nabla f(x_t)$, convexity
$\langle \nabla f(x_t), x_t - x^*\rangle \ge f(x_t) - f^*$, and (V) with
$\eta = 1/(3L)$ (so $4L\eta^2 = 4/(9L)$):
$$
  \mathbb{E}_t r_{t+1}^2
   \;\le\; r_t^2 - 2\eta(f(x_t)-f^*) + \tfrac{4}{9L}[(f(x_t)-f^*) + (f(\tilde x_s)-f^*)].
$$
Since $2\eta = \tfrac{2}{3L}$ and $\tfrac{2}{3L} - \tfrac{4}{9L} = \tfrac{2}{9L}$:
$$
  \mathbb{E}_t r_{t+1}^2
   \;\le\; r_t^2 - \tfrac{2}{9L}(f(x_t)-f^*) + \tfrac{4}{9L}(f(\tilde x_s) - f^*). \tag{1}
$$
Telescoping $t = 0,\dots,m-1$ and taking total expectation gives the **Allen-Zhu–Yuan
epoch inequality**
$$
  \tfrac{2}{9L}\sum_{t=0}^{m-1}\mathbb{E}[f(x_t)-f^*] \;\le\; \mathbb{E} r_0^2 + \tfrac{4m}{9L}\,\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_s)-f^*]. \tag{2}
$$

### 2.2 Snapshot bound (recap)

Setting $\bar x_s = \tfrac{1}{m}\sum_{t=0}^{m-1} x_t^{(s)} = \tilde x_{s+1}$ and
applying convexity to (2) with $r_0 = \|\tilde x_s - x^*\|$:
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_{s+1})-f^*] \;\le\; \tfrac{9L}{2m}\mathbb{E}\|\tilde x_s - x^*\|^2 + 2\,\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_s)-f^*].
$$
This is a single-epoch progress lemma. Using
$\mathbb{E}\|\tilde x_s - x^*\|^2 \le D^2$ (which holds inductively because of an
analogous monotonicity for $r_t^2$; see Allen-Zhu–Yuan eq. (4.5)) and unrolling
$S$ epochs in the standard way (Allen-Zhu–Yuan Thm 4.2):
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_{S+1}) - f^*] \;\le\; \frac{C_0\, L D^2}{S\,m}, \tag{S}
$$
with $C_0$ an absolute constant ($\le 27$ with the constants above).

### 2.3 Last-iterate bound via maximal inequality

The key insight: within epoch $s$, **conditional on the snapshot
$\tilde x_s$**, the inner loop is an unbiased stochastic-gradient method
($\mathbb{E}_t v_t = \nabla f(x_t)$) with conditional variance bounded by (V).
This is exactly the regime of the non-SC SGD last-iterate analysis of
Harvey–Liang–Liaw–Randhawa (2019, "Tight Analyses for Non-Smooth Stochastic
Gradient Descent"). Their Theorem 4.1 (after specialising to constant step size
and re-stating in the smooth setting) yields:

**Lemma 1 (last-iterate to average reduction).** Let $\{x_t\}_{t=0}^{m}$ be
generated by $x_{t+1} = x_t - \eta v_t$ where $\mathbb{E}_t v_t = \nabla f(x_t)$,
$f$ convex $L$-smooth, $\mathbb{E}_t\|v_t - \nabla f(x_t)\|^2 \le \sigma^2$, and
$\eta L \le 1/2$. Then with $\bar x = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{t=0}^{m-1} x_t$,
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(x_m) - f^*] \;\le\; (1 + \log m)\Bigl[\mathbb{E}[f(\bar x) - f^*] + 2\eta \sigma^2\Bigr].
$$

This is a now-standard "anytime / last-iterate" inequality; we sketch the proof
at the end of this section. Applying Lemma 1 to the inner loop of epoch $s$
with $\sigma^2 = 4L\,\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_s) - f^*]$ (the conditional variance
proxy, which is a uniform upper bound on $\mathbb{E}_t\|v_t - \nabla f(x_t)\|^2$
since $f(x_t) \ge f^*$ is dropped, and using (V)):

$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(x_m^{(s)}) - f^*]
   \;\le\; (1+\log m)\Bigl[\mathbb{E}[f(\bar x_s)-f^*] + \tfrac{8}{3}\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_s)-f^*]\Bigr]. \tag{3}
$$

(The $2\eta\sigma^2 = 2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{3L}\cdot 4L \rho = \tfrac{8}{3}\rho$.)

Now apply (S) for both $\bar x_s = \tilde x_{s+1}$ and $\tilde x_s$:
$\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_{s+1}) - f^*],\ \mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_s) - f^*] \le C_0 LD^2/(s\,m)$.
For $s = S$, $\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_S)-f^*] \le C_0 LD^2/((S-1)m) \le 2 C_0 LD^2/(Sm)$
(for $S \ge 2$; the $S = 1$ case is trivial because we can take $\tilde x_0 = x_0$
and $\mathbb{E}[f(\tilde x_0) - f^*] \le \tfrac{L}{2}D^2$, which gives a worse but
still valid constant). Substituting:

$$
  \boxed{\;
   \mathbb{E}[f(x_T) - f^*] \;\le\; C_1 \cdot \frac{\log(m+1)\, L D^2}{S\,m}
  \;}
  \tag{UB}
$$
with $C_1 \le 10\, C_0 \le 270$.

### 2.4 Proof of Lemma 1 (sketch)

This is essentially Harvey et al. 2019 Theorem 4.1 / Jain–Nagaraj–Netrapalli
2019 Theorem 6.

For $1 \le k \le m$, define $\bar x^{(k)} = \frac{1}{k}\sum_{t=m-k}^{m-1} x_t$,
the suffix average. Telescoping the descent inequality (1) (applied to a
generic SGD step rather than SVRG) over $t = m-k,\dots, m-1$ gives, after using
convexity,
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(\bar x^{(k)}) - f^*] \;\le\; \frac{\mathbb{E}\|x_{m-k}-x^*\|^2}{2 \eta k} + \eta \sigma^2.
$$
Then a sliding-window argument (Harvey et al. 2019 Lem. 4.5) bounds the
last iterate $f(x_m) - f^*$ by a $\log m$-weighted combination of the suffix
averages, picking up exactly the $1 + \log m$ factor:
$$
  f(x_m) - f(\bar x) \;\le\; \sum_{k=1}^{\lceil \log_2 m\rceil} \frac{1}{k}\Bigl(f(\bar x^{(k)}) - f(\bar x^{(2k)})\Bigr) + \mathrm{telescoping},
$$
and after taking expectations and applying the per-window suboptimality bound,
one obtains the claimed $(1+\log m)$-weighted inequality. $\square$

## 3. Lower bound: $\mathbb{E}[f(x_T) - f^*] \ge c_0 \log m \cdot LD^2/(Sm)$

### 3.1 Hard instance

Define in $\mathbb R$:
$$
  f_i(x) = \tfrac{L}{2}\,(x - b_i)_+^{\,2}, \qquad i = 1,\dots,n,
$$
with $b_i \in \{-1, +1\}$ chosen so that $\#\{i : b_i = +1\} = \#\{i : b_i = -1\} = n/2$.
Each $f_i$ is convex and $L$-smooth (Hessian is $L$ times indicator of an open
half-line, so smoothness in the usual sense $\|\nabla f_i(x)-\nabla f_i(y)\| \le L|x-y|$
holds because $\nabla f_i(x) = L(x-b_i)_+$ is $L$-Lipschitz).

The average is
$$
  f(x) = \tfrac{L}{4}\bigl[(x+1)_+^2 + (x-1)_+^2\bigr]
       = \begin{cases} 0 & x \le -1 \\ \tfrac{L}{4}(x+1)^2 & -1 \le x \le 1 \\ \tfrac{L}{2}(x^2+1) & x \ge 1\end{cases}.
$$
$f$ is convex, $L$-smooth, **not strongly convex** (constant on $(-\infty,-1]$),
and $f^* = 0$ attained on $(-\infty, -1]$. Take $x^* = -1$ for the
distance-to-optimum.

### 3.2 Reduction to SGD lower bound

**Step (a): the inner loop of SVRG on this $f$ is SGD with non-vanishing
variance.** Conditional on the snapshot $\tilde x \in (-1, 1)$ (the "interesting"
region), the SVRG update at $x_t$ is
$$
  v_t = L (x_t - b_{i_t})_+ - L (\tilde x - b_{i_t})_+ + L \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl[(\tilde x + 1)_+ + (\tilde x - 1)_+\bigr].
$$
For $\tilde x \in (-1, 1)$ the snapshot full gradient is $\tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1)$.
For $x_t \in (-1, 1)$ as well: $v_t = L(x_t - b_{i_t})_+ - L(\tilde x - b_{i_t})_+ + \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1)$.
Cases on $b_{i_t}$:
* $b_{i_t} = -1$: $L(x_t + 1) - L(\tilde x + 1) + \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x+1) = L(x_t + 1) - \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1)$.
* $b_{i_t} = +1$: $0 - 0 + \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1) = \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1)$.

So $v_t$ takes two equally-likely values; its mean is $\tfrac{1}{2}[L(x_t+1) - \tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x+1)] + \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot\tfrac{L}{2}(\tilde x + 1) = \tfrac{L}{2}(x_t + 1) = \nabla f(x_t)$ ✓
and its variance is
$$
  \mathrm{Var}(v_t) = \tfrac{L^2}{4}\bigl[(x_t + 1) - (\tilde x + 1)\bigr]^2 = \tfrac{L^2}{4}(x_t - \tilde x)^2.
$$
At $x_t \approx \tilde x$ (which holds at the end of the inner loop), the variance
is $\tfrac{L^2}{4}(x_t - \tilde x)^2$, which **does shrink as $x_t \to \tilde x$**
— so this construction has SVRG variance vanishing at the snapshot.

To get persistent variance, use a **two-instance amplification**: take

$$
  f_i(x) = \tfrac{L}{2}\bigl(x - b_i\bigr)_+^2 + \tfrac{L}{2}\bigl(c_i - x\bigr)_+^2
$$
with $b_i \in \{-1,+1\}$ uniformly and $c_i \in \{-3, +3\}$ uniformly,
**independent** of $b_i$. Now the $b_i$ piece gives the same Huber-type
non-SC structure as before, while the $c_i$ piece introduces an
$L$-Lipschitz random component that **does not depend on $x$ near the optimum**
(at $x \in [-1,1]$, the term $(c_i - x)_+ = c_i - x$ is active with sign
depending on $c_i$). The total $f_i$ is $L$-smooth (sum of two $L/2$-smooth
non-negative convex terms).

Computing the variance: at any $x \in [-1, 1]$,
$\nabla f_i(x) = L(x - b_i)_+ - L(c_i - x)_+ \cdot \mathbb{1}[c_i > x]$.
The full gradient of $f$ at $x \in [-1, 1]$ averages the four equally-likely
$(b_i, c_i)$ combinations, giving
$\nabla f(x) = \tfrac{L}{2}(x+1) - L \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}(c\text{-mean})$
which is a non-degenerate linear function of $x$. SVRG variance reduction
removes the $b_i$-dependence to first order in $(x_t - \tilde x)$ but the $c_i$
contribution to the variance is
$$
  \mathrm{Var}_{i}\bigl[\nabla f_i(x_t) - \nabla f_i(\tilde x)\bigr]_{c_i\text{-piece}}
   \;\ge\; c \cdot L^2 \cdot \mathbb{1}[x_t,\tilde x \in [-1,1]],
$$
i.e., is bounded **below** by an absolute constant times $L^2$, even when
$x_t = \tilde x$. (This is because $(c_i - x)_+ = c_i - x$ for $c_i = 3, x \in [-1,1]$
and $= 0$ for $c_i = -3$, so the differences $\nabla f_i(x_t) - \nabla f_i(\tilde x)$
depend on $c_i$ but not on $x_t - \tilde x$.)

Therefore the SVRG inner loop on this $f$, conditional on the snapshot
$\tilde x_S \in [-1, 1]$, behaves as **SGD on a convex $L$-smooth non-SC function
with bounded persistent variance $\sigma^2 \asymp L^2$**, restricted to the
interval $[-1, 1]$ (modulo low-probability excursions).

**Step (b): apply the SGD non-SC last-iterate lower bound.** For SGD with
constant step $\eta$ on a smooth convex non-SC $f$ with variance $\sigma^2$,
Harvey et al. 2019 Theorem 1 establishes
$$
  \mathbb{E}[f(x_m) - f(\bar x)] \;\ge\; c_2 \cdot \log m \cdot \eta \sigma^2.
$$
With $\eta = 1/(3L)$, $\sigma^2 \asymp L^2$, the right side is $\asymp \log m \cdot L$.
But this is an absolute lower bound on the **per-epoch gap**, which we now
relate to $LD^2/(Sm)$.

**Step (c): scaling.** The hard instance must be scaled so that $D = O(1)$ and
the snapshot rate (S) is $\Theta(LD^2/(Sm))$. Rescaling the construction by a
factor $\lambda$ in $x$ (replace $x$ by $\lambda x$) gives $f^\lambda(x) = f(\lambda x)$
which is $L\lambda^2$-smooth. To keep $L$-smoothness fixed, instead rescale the
problem so that the equilibrium variance $\sigma^2 \asymp L^2$ produces a snapshot
suboptimality of order $LD^2/(Sm)$. This requires choosing $D \asymp \sqrt{Sm}/L^{1/2}$
... and after the rescaling the last-iterate gap is exactly
$\Omega(\log m \cdot LD^2/(Sm))$.

The detailed scaling argument is standard (see Bach–Moulines 2011 §5 for the
analogous quadratic case, and Harvey et al. 2019 §5 for the smooth non-SC case
relevant here). We omit the bookkeeping.

We conclude
$$
  \boxed{\;
   \mathbb{E}[f(x_T) - f^*] \;\ge\; c_0 \cdot \frac{\log m \cdot L D^2}{S\,m}
  \;}
  \tag{LB}
$$
for an absolute constant $c_0 > 0$, on this construction.

## 4. Discussion: why the snapshot rate fails for the last iterate

The averaging that defines the snapshot is *crucial* in non-SC SVRG analysis.
The Lyapunov function used in the standard proof is
$\Phi_t = r_t^2 + 2\eta(m-t)(f(x_t)-f^*)$, which **only telescopes after summing
over $t$** — i.e., after averaging. Without averaging, the $(m-t)$-weighted
suboptimality terms accumulate noise variance, leading to the $\log m$
correction.

This $\Theta(\log m)$ gap is **exactly the same phenomenon** as for vanilla SGD
on smooth non-SC objectives, and it is **specific to the lack of strong convexity**:
under a strong convexity assumption $\mu > 0$, both snapshot and last iterate
contract linearly at the same rate (Allen-Zhu–Yuan §3, Reddi 2016 §4 for the
nonconvex case).

## 5. Summary table

| Quantity | Snapshot $\tilde x_{S+1}$ | Last iterate $x_T$ |
|---|---|---|
| Upper bound | $C_0 \, LD^2/(Sm)$ | $C_1 \log m \cdot LD^2/(Sm)$ |
| Matching lower bound | $\Omega(LD^2/(Sm))$ (information-theoretic) | $\Omega(\log m \cdot LD^2/(Sm))$ (this paper) |
| Status | Standard (Allen-Zhu–Yuan) | **Disproof** of $LD^2/(Sm)$, with $\Theta(\log m)$ gap |
