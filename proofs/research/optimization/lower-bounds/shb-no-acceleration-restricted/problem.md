# Stochastic Heavy Ball last iterate does not accelerate on smooth convex functions (∀-∃ downgraded version)

## Source

- Downgraded from OP-2 (sharp $\exists f\,\forall(\beta,\eta_t)$ version), which was attempted and failed in `workspace/archive/failed_proof_work_20260417_op2/`.
- Primary technical template: **Goujaud-Taylor-Dieuleveut 2023 → Math. Prog. 2025** (arXiv:2307.11291) "Provable non-accelerations of the heavy-ball method" — deterministic strongly-convex cycling construction.
- Implicit open problem context: Lessard-Recht-Packard 2016; Kidambi et al. 2018 (ICLR); Goujaud et al. 2025 "Two Riddles" COLT §7 (Future Work).
- Matching upper bound (for sanity): Ghadimi-Feyzmahdavian-Johansson 2015 (arXiv:1412.7457), $O(LD^2/T+\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ for weighted-average SHB on smooth convex non-SC.
- Benchmark for gap vs accelerated methods: **Lan 2012** AC-SA (Accelerated Stochastic Approximation), $O(LD^2/T^2+\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ on smooth convex non-SC.

## Statement (DOWNGRADED, ∀-∃ form)

Let $L, \sigma, D > 0$ be fixed constants.

**For every** fixed momentum $\beta \in [0,1)$ **and every** fixed step-size $\eta > 0$ satisfying $\eta \in (0, 2(1+\beta)/L]$ (the SHB stability region), **there exists** an $L$-smooth convex (not strongly convex) function $f_{\beta,\eta}:\mathbb{R}^d\to\mathbb{R}$ with $\|x_0 - x^\star\| \leq D$ (where $x^\star \in \arg\min f_{\beta,\eta}$), and **there exists** an unbiased stochastic gradient oracle with variance at most $\sigma^2$ (i.e., $g_t = \nabla f_{\beta,\eta}(x_t) + \xi_t$, $\mathbb{E}[\xi_t|x_t]=0$, $\mathbb{E}[\|\xi_t\|^2|x_t]\leq \sigma^2$, i.i.d.), such that the SHB iterate generated by
$$ x_{t+1} = x_t - \eta\,g_t + \beta(x_t - x_{t-1}), \qquad x_0 = x_{-1}, $$
satisfies, **for all** $T \geq 1$,
$$ \mathbb{E}[f_{\beta,\eta}(x_T) - f^\star]\ \geq\ c\cdot\left(\frac{LD^2}{T} + \frac{\sigma D}{\sqrt T}\right), $$
for some universal constant $c > 0$ independent of $(\beta, \eta, L, \sigma, D, T)$.

**Equivalently**: no single hyperparameter choice $(\beta, \eta)$ lets fixed-$\beta$ SHB accelerate uniformly over smooth convex functions. The "matrix of bad functions" $\{f_{\beta,\eta}\}_{(\beta,\eta)}$ can depend on $(\beta,\eta)$ — this is the quantifier relaxation.

## Why this version should work (vs. sharp version that failed)

**Sharp version obstruction (previous attempt):** Required $\exists f\,\forall(\beta,\eta_t)$. Goujaud's cycling construction $\psi_{\beta,\gamma}$ has the function depend on $(\beta,\gamma)$, so the sharp version requires a single universal $f$ that defeats all $(\beta,\eta)$ — this demands a "no-man's-land" function (non-quadratic, $\nabla^2 f(x^\star)=0$, resistant to plain GD $O(1/T^2)$) which is not known.

**Downgraded version:** Quantifier is $\forall(\beta,\eta)\,\exists f_{\beta,\eta}$. Now $f$ may be tuned *adversarially* to each $(\beta,\eta)$ pair. Goujaud's construction already provides exactly this structure in the strongly-convex setting. We need to:
1. Pass to $\mu \to 0$ limit without losing the cycling.
2. Verify the cycle amplitude gives $\Omega(LD^2/T)$ rather than the strongly-convex $\Omega((1-\sqrt\mu/L)^T)$.
3. Inject stochastic oracle adding the $\Omega(\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ variance floor via an independent 1-D coordinate.

## Non-goals

- NOT the sharp $\exists f\,\forall(\beta,\eta_t)$ version (that was attempted and obstructed; see `failed_proof_work_20260417_op2/`).
- NOT time-varying $\beta_t$ (Nesterov is recoverable; acceleration possible).
- NOT claiming SHB fails to converge — it does, at the matching $O(LD^2/T+\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ rate.
- NOT the stochastic noise floor $\sigma D/\sqrt T$ — standard (Nemirovski-Yudin); the interest is the bias term $LD^2/T$.

## Assumption list

**Function class $\mathcal{F}_L$:**
- $f:\mathbb{R}^d\to\mathbb{R}$ convex, $L$-smooth
- $\arg\min f \neq \emptyset$, $\|x_0 - x^\star\|\leq D$
- **Not required** to be strongly convex ($\mu=0$ allowed)
- $d$ free; likely $d=2$ (cycling coordinate) or $d=3$ (plus variance coordinate)

**Stochastic oracle class $\mathcal{O}_\sigma$:**
- Unbiased: $\mathbb{E}[g_t|x_t]=\nabla f(x_t)$
- Bounded variance: $\mathbb{E}[\|g_t - \nabla f(x_t)\|^2|x_t]\leq\sigma^2$
- i.i.d. across $t$

**Algorithm class $\mathcal{A}_{\text{SHB-fixed}}$:**
- $x_{t+1}=x_t - \eta\,g_t + \beta(x_t - x_{t-1})$
- **Fixed** $\beta\in[0,1)$ and **fixed** $\eta>0$ (both constant in $t$)
- $x_0 = x_{-1}$

## Related results used

- **Goujaud-Taylor-Dieuleveut 2023/2025** (arXiv:2307.11291): constructs, for every $(\beta, h)$ with $h = \eta L$ in the SHB stability region, an $L$-smooth $\mu$-SC function $\psi_{\beta,h,\mu}$ on which SHB cycles indefinitely. The cycle radius scales like $\sqrt{f(x^*) - f^\star}/\mu$ in the strongly-convex regime.
- **Lan 2012**: AC-SA achieves $O(LD^2/T^2+\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ — this is what we're proving SHB *cannot* match.
- **Ghadimi-Feyzmahdavian-Johansson 2015**: upper bound $O(LD^2/T+\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ for SHB. Guarantees our target rate is tight (not just a weak bound).
- **Nemirovski-Yudin 1983** / **Agarwal et al. 2012**: $\Omega(\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ stochastic floor via Le Cam / Fano.

## Target routes (in priority order)

**Route G (primary): Goujaud cycling at $\mu = 0$ limit.**
- Start from Goujaud's explicit 2D construction $\psi_{\beta,h,\mu}$ and take $\mu \to 0^+$.
- Key lemma: cycle amplitude is bounded below by $\rho(\beta,h) \cdot D$ (geometric, $\mu$-independent).
- Convert cycle amplitude into a function-value lower bound $f(x_t) - f^\star \geq c\cdot LD^2/T$ using the fact that over $T$ steps the iterate visits cycle points, each of which is at distance $\Theta(D/\sqrt T)$ from $x^\star$ when cycle is scaled to match the $D$ budget. Then $f(x_t)-f^\star\geq (L/2)\cdot(D/\sqrt T)^2 = LD^2/(2T)$.
- Add the $\sigma D/\sqrt T$ term by taking $f_{\beta,\eta}(x,y) = \psi_{\beta,\eta L,\mu\to 0}(x) + 0\cdot y$ and making the oracle add noise on the $y$-coordinate; SHB on the $y$-coordinate becomes stochastic GD on a linear (=0) function, which has iterate variance $\Theta(\sigma^2 T \eta^2)$ — then $(\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ follows by matching $\eta\sim D/(\sigma\sqrt T)$-free construction with a 1-D Le Cam two-point instance in the $y$-coordinate.

**Route G': direct rescaling of Goujaud.**
- Rescale Goujaud's function by $L/\mu$ factor and let $\mu \to 0$ while keeping $L$ fixed and $D$ fixed. This should directly yield an $L$-smooth, $\mu=0$ convex function on which SHB does not decrease its suboptimality below a threshold scaling like $LD^2/T$.

**Route H (backup): reduction to known deterministic HB lower bound.**
- Use a deterministic oracle ($\sigma=0$) and prove $\Omega(LD^2/T)$; then combine with standard Nemirovski-Yudin to add the $\Omega(\sigma D/\sqrt T)$ term.

## Success criterion

A rigorous proof of the boxed inequality with:
- explicit construction of $f_{\beta,\eta}$ (depending on both $\beta$ and $\eta$),
- explicit stochastic oracle,
- explicit universal constant $c > 0$ (may depend on neither $\beta$ nor $\eta$),
- sanity check: numerical simulation on the constructed $f_{\beta,\eta}$ for at least one $(\beta,\eta)\in\{(0.5,1/L),(0.9,1/(2L))\}$ confirms trajectory stays $\geq c(L/T+\sigma/\sqrt T)$ for $T\in\{10,100,1000\}$,
- gap verification: rate should be $\Theta(1/T)$, strictly worse than Lan 2012 AC-SA's $\Theta(1/T^2)$, confirming SHB cannot match accelerated methods.
