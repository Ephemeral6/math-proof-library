# LDT Auditor Checklist

> Created: 2026-04-20 (Stage 1.5 of LDT extension)
>
> This checklist is the LDT-specific complement to `math-proof-agent/prompts/auditor.md`. It does NOT replace the V2 auditor; it lives alongside it. When auditing an LDT proof, run both: V2 auditor produces the `## Audit Report` + `## Constant Tracing` + `## Cross-Verification` sections; this checklist produces the additional `## LDT-Specific Audit` section.
>

## When to apply this checklist

Trigger: the problem.md is tagged with the LDT domain OR any of the five keywords (纽结理论 / 映射类群 / 曲线复形 / Teichmüller 理论 / 三维流形). Otherwise skip and use the standard V2 auditor only.

## LDT-specific audit items

For each item below, the auditor must output one of `OK / FLAG / N/A` and a 1-sentence justification.

### A. Isotopy vs. equivalence distinction

Different notions of "sameness" for knots, links, surfaces, and maps:
1. **Ambient isotopy** — the natural notion for knots in S^3
2. **Regular isotopy** — invariant under R2 and R3 only (NOT R1); relevant for Kauffman bracket
3. **Isotopy rel boundary** — for arcs
4. **Homeomorphism of complements** — for knots, equivalent to ambient isotopy up to mirror image (Gordon–Luecke)
5. **Homotopy** — weaker than any of the above

**Audit requirement**: Identify every use of "equivalent" / "isotopic" / "the same" in the proof. Confirm each instance specifies WHICH notion. Flag if ambiguous.

### B. Orientation check

- Is the knot oriented or unoriented? Many invariants (HOMFLY, signature) depend on orientation.
- Mirror / reverse operations: `mirror(K)` = reflection of the diagram (changes all crossings), `reverse(K)` = opposite orientation. Some invariants are preserved, others not (e.g., Jones distinguishes K from mirror(K) generically).
- For braids: `β` and `β^{-1}` have different closures.
- For surfaces / MCG: orientation-preserving vs. full MCG matters.

**Audit requirement**: If the proof claims an invariant equality, confirm the orientation convention matches on both sides. If the proof handles a specific knot, confirm the hand (right/left) is consistent throughout.

### C. Dimension check

4-dim vs 3-dim:
- **Slice** = bounds a smooth disk in B^4 (4-dim concept)
- **Ribbon** = bounds an immersed disk in S^3 with only ribbon singularities (3-dim concept)
- Every ribbon knot is slice; the slice-ribbon conjecture asks the converse.
- **Unknotting number** (U(K)) is a 3-dim concept; **4-ball genus** (g_4(K)) is 4-dim.

**Audit requirement**: If the proof invokes slice, ribbon, concordance, or 4-manifold notions, confirm it hasn't silently dropped back to 3-dim arguments. Specific red flag: using Seifert-genus bounds as if they were 4-ball-genus bounds (they give upper bounds, not equalities).

### D. Compactness / infinitude check

- **Curve complex C(S)** is INFINITE (infinite-valence vertices and infinite dimension generically).
- **Mapping class groups** are FINITELY generated but INFINITE (except small cases).
- **Teichmüller space** is a manifold of dimension 6g−6+2n; infinitely many distinct metrics.

**Audit requirement**: If the proof uses compactness, the Bolzano–Weierstrass principle, or existence-via-limit, confirm the object being limited IS compact. Red flag: any sentence of the form "consider the finite subcomplex of C(S) containing..." without specifying HOW it's finite.

### E. Group presentation consistency

MCG has multiple standard presentations (Humphries, Wajnryb, Gervais, Luo). Different choices of generators lead to different relation lists; a proof using "MCG is generated by twists on curves c_1, ..., c_k" must fix the choice.

**Audit requirement**: Flag any step where the presentation changes mid-proof without explicit translation. Confirm that translations between presentations are done via citable results (Farb–Margalit Prop 4.8 for Humphries, etc.), not ad-hoc.

### F. Literature cross-check (V2 Pattern 4 emphasis)

Hyperbolic constants, distance bounds, and uniform-bound constants are PARTICULARLY inconsistent across LDT sources:
- C(S) hyperbolicity constant δ: quoted as 17 (Hensel–Przytycki–Webb), 4 (Bowditch), 36 (earlier Masur–Minsky), etc. — they correspond to different normalizations.
- Subsurface-projection bounded-geodesic-image constants: many versions.
- Volume bounds in 3-manifolds: Gromov norm convention vs. simplicial volume convention differ by a factor of 2.

**Audit requirement**: Every quoted constant must cite a specific paper/theorem. If the citation is vague ("a standard result says δ ≤ 17"), flag for source verification.

### F2. Citation-depth classification (L1 / L2 / L3)

> Motivation: the Probe 1 / Probe 6 finding that Round 0.5's hyperbolic proof rests on 4 E3 citations while registering as "fully justified" on Completeness. A single citation to "Gordon–Luecke 1989" and a single citation to "Thurston hyperbolization" are not equivalent loads on the proof's self-contained portion, but the base rubric treats them identically. This sub-checklist makes the load explicit.

For every `[REF:external]` citation in the proof, the Auditor classifies its **citation depth**:

- **L1 — Light**: a reader with a textbook and ~30 seconds can verify the cited fact directly (e.g., "the Alexander polynomial of the trefoil is $t^2 - t + 1$" cited to KnotInfo; "Kauffman bracket axioms" cited to Kauffman 1987). L1 citations are effectively lemma lookups.

- **L2 — Theorem-grade**: the cited statement is a single theorem from a single paper/textbook; verifying it requires reading that paper but not a separate research program (e.g., "Alexander's theorem: every link is a braid closure" from Alexander 1923; "Markov's theorem" from Markov 1935; "Bennequin's inequality"; "Birman–Hilden for hyperelliptic covers").

- **L3 — Deep machinery**: the cited result is itself a significant theorem whose proof spans a monograph or a research program. Removing the citation would gut the proof; the Explorer is NOT reconstructing the machinery, only invoking its name.

**Canonical L3 reference list (low-dimensional-topology)**:

- Thurston's hyperbolization theorem (Haken case and Perelman's full case).
- Mostow rigidity theorem.
- Gordon–Luecke theorem (knots determined by complements).
- Agol's theorem on the virtually Haken / virtual fibering conjectures.
- Wise's quasi-convex hierarchy machinery.
- Masur–Minsky curve complex machinery (hyperbolicity of $C(S)$, tight geodesics, subsurface projections, bounded geodesic image, hierarchical hyperbolicity).
- Mirzakhani's moduli-space volume recursion / Weil–Petersson dynamics.
- Kirby calculus / Kirby's theorem (4-manifold handle moves).
- Heegaard Floer homology (Ozsváth–Szabó), its tau / Upsilon concordance invariants.
- Khovanov homology, Lee homology, Rasmussen's s-invariant.
- Thurston's 8-geometry classification for 3-manifolds.
- Eskin–Mirzakhani–Mohammadi / Eskin–Mirzakhani rigidity of $SL(2,\mathbb{R})$-orbit closures.

(An L3 citation OUTSIDE this list is still an L3 if it meets the "deep machinery" bar; the list is seed, not exhaustive.)

**Auditor procedure**:

1. For every `[REF:external]` in the proof, tag it `L1`, `L2`, or `L3`.
2. Record the list of L3 citations in a new audit sub-section `### L3 citations used`.
3. Count Independent steps $N_I$: steps the Explorer derived without an external citation (label `[IND]` or absence of `[REF:]`).
4. **STRUCTURAL-CITATION-WARNING trigger**: if the proof has **≥3 L3 citations AND <3 Independent steps**, emit
   ```
   [WARN: STRUCTURAL-CITATION-WARNING]
     L3 citations: N ≥ 3 (listed above)
     Independent steps: M < 3
     Finding: the proof's logical skeleton rests primarily on deep external
     machinery; the Explorer's contribution is routing between cited
     theorems rather than reconstructing their content. Verdict may still
     be PASS if every citation is load-appropriate, but the proof should
     be archived with this caveat so the collaborator knows the rubric's
     Completeness score is not equivalent to mathematical originality.
   ```
5. The STRUCTURAL-CITATION-WARNING does NOT override a PASS → it appears alongside the verdict. A heavily-cited proof that is otherwise correct should pass; the warning just prevents the Completeness-insensitive scoring mismatch (diagnostic finding 6) from being silent.

**Output location**: the `### L3 citations used` sub-section goes into the `## LDT-Specific Audit` block between `### Geometric Intuition Assessment` and `### LDT-specific issues found`. If zero L3 citations are present, write `### L3 citations used\nNone.` explicitly (absence should be visible in the audit trail).

**Note on non-LDT proofs**: the L-taxonomy is domain-agnostic in principle, but the canonical L3 list above is LDT-specific. A future extension should seed similar lists for optimization (e.g., Nesterov's accelerated method is L2; KKT regularity analysis at the level of Robinson is L3), learning theory (e.g., VC covering bounds are L2; Talagrand's generic-chaining machinery is L3), etc. For now, the L-classification is only mandatory inside the LDT checklist.

### F3. Verified-Sympy Block protocol compliance

*Added: 2026-04-21 (pipeline v2.2-P1).* Triggered whenever the proof carries
at least one `[VERIFIED-SYMPY:...]` tag, or whenever a proof step makes a
finite-parameter empirical claim ("verified for $p \le 6$", "checked 62
cases", etc.) without such a tag.

See `~/.claude/skills/math-verifier/VERIFIED_SYMPY_PROTOCOL.md` for the full
protocol. Auditor procedure for F3:

1. **Tag present but script missing** (HARD FAIL): a tag cites `script=<path>`
   that does not exist in the work directory. Report as an audit error; Fixer
   must either produce the script or remove the claim.

2. **Script missing protocol block**: tag cites a script that does exist, but
   the script lacks the `[VERIFIED-SYMPY-PROTOCOL: <template-id>, ...]` line
   in its docstring. Report as FLAG; Fixer adds the block.

3. **Case-count mismatch**: the `cases=<N>` field in the inline tag does not
   match the integer printed on the script's final "ALL PASSED" line. Report
   as FLAG; the inline tag must be updated.

4. **Script not deterministic / uses floats / uses randomness**: report as
   FLAG; Fixer converts to sympy symbolic equality.

5. **Script exceeds 60s on a single core**: report as FLAG; Fixer splits
   into smaller parameter ranges.

6. **Empirical claim without a tag** (FLAG): a step of the form "verified
   for $n \le N$" or "checked $M$ cases" appears in the proof body without
   an accompanying `[VERIFIED-SYMPY:...]` tag. Report the step and require
   Fixer to either add the tag or re-phrase the claim to be purely symbolic.

7. **Scope overreach** (HARD FAIL): the sympy tag cites a universal claim
   ("for all $n \ge 0$, $X(n)$ holds") and has only finite verification.
   Finite verification is necessary but not sufficient; the symbolic proof
   must still be in the body. Report as a reliance mismatch.

Output: a subsection `### F3 Verified-Sympy compliance` inside the
`## LDT-Specific Audit` block, listing each finding or `None.` if all tags
and claims are protocol-compliant.

### G. Picture-proof handling (CRITICAL)

When Auditor encounters a handwave or picture argument (e.g., "by looking at the diagram, the twists commute"):

**Procedure**:
1. Flag the step as `[PICTURE-ARGUMENT]`.
2. Attempt TSV verification:
   - For Dehn-twist relations → `tsv_group.check_braid_between_twists` or `check_commuting_disjoint_twists`
   - For knot-equivalence claims → `tsv_knot.check_reidemeister_equivalent`
   - For curve-complex distance claims → `tsv_simplicial.verify_simplicial_distance_upper_bound`
3. If TSV confirms → step becomes `[VERIFIED: tsv-group|tsv-knot|tsv-simplicial, confidence=high]`, remove `[PICTURE-ARGUMENT]` tag.
4. If TSV is out-of-scope → Pass to Fixer with explicit instruction: "try to re-formulate via available TSV; if impossible, leave marked `[UNVERIFIABLE: picture]` and degrade verdict to PARTIAL."

**System policy**: A proof with more than 2 `[UNVERIFIABLE: picture]` tags CANNOT receive a PASS verdict. Maximum acceptable is PARTIAL with honest archive.

### H. Collaborator evaluation criterion

The collaborator explicitly stated: **"希望看到 LLM 具有几何直觉，而不是只有计算的能力"** ("want to see the LLM have geometric intuition, not just computational ability").

**Audit requirement**: In the `## LDT-Specific Audit` summary, add a subsection `### Geometric Intuition Assessment` scoring the proof on a 0–5 scale:
- 0: pure symbol-pushing; no geometric insight visible.
- 2: reasoning is correct but treats knots/surfaces as abstract algebraic objects.
- 4: proof explicitly uses geometric vocabulary (picture, isotopy class, intersection number) and ties computations back to geometric meaning.
- 5: proof exhibits geometric insight that produces a simpler argument than the purely algebraic one (e.g., using the hyperbolic structure of 4_1's complement to conclude non-equivalence, not just Jones polynomials).

This score goes into the archived `notes.md` and is reported back to the collaborator.

## Notes for future iterations

- This checklist is fresh. Items A–H are hypotheses from the task brief. After Round 0, tighten any item that didn't trigger when it should have, and drop any item that never triggers (but only after observing several LDT proofs — don't drop items prematurely).
- If "picture-proof handling" is catching too many false positives (i.e., Explorer is producing picture arguments that are easily verifiable), consider demoting from CRITICAL to a normal flag.
- Consider adding a Item I: "surface-complexity check" (ensuring 3g−3+n ≥ 1 so C(S) is connected, etc.) once a proof actually involves a complexity-dependent step.
