# Corollary 3.10

## Statement
Let $S(p,q,\epsilon)$ and $S(p',q',\epsilon')$ be spiral knots. If
$S(p,q,\epsilon) \cong S(p',q',\epsilon')$ as oriented knots in $S^3$,
then their Alexander polynomials agree up to units in $\mathbb Z[t,t^{-1}]$:
$$
\Delta_{S(p,q,\epsilon)}(t) \doteq \Delta_{S(p',q',\epsilon')}(t).
$$

## Source
- Paper: Blackwell, Chen, et al. arXiv:2506.17889
- Location: §3, Corollary 3.10.

## Status
UNPROVEN-HERE

## Usage contract
- Citation tag: [REF:level1:corollary_3_10]
- Only the forward direction (equivalent knots ⇒ equal Alexander
  polynomials). The converse (equal Alexander ⇒ equivalent) is FALSE
  in general (Conway knot, etc.).

## Notes
Standard knot-theory fact: the Alexander polynomial is an ambient-
isotopy invariant. Does NOT claim the Alexander polynomial is a
COMPLETE invariant for spiral knots. A route that treats Δ-equality as
determining knot equivalence is making a stronger claim than this
corollary supports; such routes should be flagged STRATEGIC if they
cannot recover the converse from additional structure.
