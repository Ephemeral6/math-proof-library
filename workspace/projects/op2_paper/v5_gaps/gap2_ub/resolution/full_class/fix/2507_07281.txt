                                         with bounded second derivative and with finite variance in the noise. Specifically, under such
                                         conditions, results on almost sure convergence, scaling limit in distribution, and L2 convergence
                                         rates have been derived [3, theorem 1-2,4,6]. Similar results can also be found for the special
                                         case (1.2). Such a scheme is a class of Robbins-Monro procedures [14]. See also [6]. Under
                                         some conditions on the noise and F , Robbins-Monro type algorithms converge almost surely [10,
                                         section I.1, thm I.9]. Such a statement relies on the Robbins-Siegmund theorem [13, theorem
                                         1], which is a convergence theorem for non-negative almost super-martingale. We remark that
                                         other methods to treat the almost sure convergence of Robbins-Monro algorithm exists, e.g. the
                                         Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) method , which relies on the stability theory of ODE.
                                         See [10, section I, page 11].
